# AdS7 x S4 Geometry: Near-Horizon of M5-Branes

The **near-horizon geometry** of a stack of N coincident M5-branes is **AdS₇ × S⁴** — a key example of the AdS/CFT holographic duality in M-theory.

#### Core Geometry
- **AdS₇**: 7-dimensional Anti-de Sitter space — maximally symmetric with negative curvature.
- **S⁴**: 4-dimensional sphere — positive curvature, radius related to Planck length and N.
- **Flux**: 4-form flux through S⁴ proportional to N (number of M5-branes).

#### Key Parameters
- Radius of AdS₇ and S⁴: \( R = ( \pi N )^{1/3} l_p \)
- Curvature scale set by brane charge N
- 11D = AdS₇ (7D) + S⁴ (4D) + time

#### Holographic Dual
- Bulk: 11D supergravity on AdS₇ × S⁴
- Boundary: 6D (2,0) superconformal field theory on stack of N M5-branes
- Strong-weak duality: large N → classical gravity, small N → strongly coupled CFT

#### Physical Meaning
- Gravity in the bulk encodes the quantum CFT on the boundary.
- No known Lagrangian for (2,0) theory — defined holographically via AdS₇.
